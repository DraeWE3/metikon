METIKON ENGINEERING LIMITED PROJECT DELIVERED 2022
LAGOS, NIGERIA

The Project

In 2022, Metikon Engineering Limited completed the installation and commissioning of a full-facade Building Integrated Photovoltaic (BIPV) system on Sterling Towers — the 17-storey headquarters of Sterling Bank Plc. on Marina Road, Lagos Island. The project represents the largest BIPV integration on any building in Africa, and the first of its kind in Nigeria.

The scope covered the integration of over 3,000 crystalline silicon photovoltaic glass modules across approximately 6,500 square metres of the tower's spandrel facades, delivering a system capacity of 955 kWp — roughly 1 megawatt peak of clean power. The system is paired with a 2.18 MWh containerized battery energy storage system, enabling the building to operate independently of the national grid during daylight hours.

Metikon served as the principal BIPV installer and electrical engineering and power systems contractor for the project, managing the full integration lifecycle from facade module installation through to battery storage commissioning and grid-tie configuration.

Why This Project Matters

The Sterling Towers BIPV installation is not a rooftop solar array bolted onto an existing structure. It is a deep integration — the photovoltaic glass is the building envelope. The panels replace conventional facade materials entirely, turning the tower's exterior walls into a power-generating surface. This is the fundamental distinction of BIPV: the building does not merely carry solar panels; it becomes the power plant.

Over its 25-year projected lifespan, the system is expected to generate 10,500 MWh of clean electricity, eliminate over 7,000 metric tonnes of carbon emissions, and displace the consumption of approximately 1 million litres of diesel fuel.

In Lagos — a city where electricity supply remains unreliable and diesel generation remains the default for commercial buildings — an installation of this scale carries weight beyond its technical specifications. It demonstrates that energy independence for large commercial structures is achievable, and that the economics of BIPV can compete with (and eventually outperform) the cost of diesel dependency.

Metikon's Role

Metikon Engineering Limited executed this project as one of very few firms in Africa qualified to handle BIPV integration at this scale. Our scope encompassed the full electrical and power systems engineering: facade module integration, inverter and combiner box architecture, DC and AC distribution, battery energy storage commissioning, and grid-tie configuration.

The installation began in Q4 2021 and was completed in Q4 2022. The project required close coordination between structural, electrical, and facade engineering disciplines — particularly given the constraints of retrofitting an active, occupied high-rise in one of Lagos's most congested commercial districts.

Africa's BIPV Landscape

Sterling Towers now stands alongside a small but growing cohort of notable BIPV projects on the continent: the Eastgate Centre in Harare, Zimbabwe (20% solar-powered); the One Africa Place in Nairobi, Kenya (50% solar-powered); and the African Leadership University Campus in Kigali, Rwanda (100% solar-powered). Sterling Towers is the largest of these by installed PV area and system capacity, and the only one integrated onto a commercial high-rise facade at this scale.

The global BIPV market continues to expand as architects, developers, and corporate tenants recognise that building envelopes represent the single largest underutilised surface area for distributed energy generation in cities.

"The idea behind the Sterling Towers project was to build an off-grid, futuristic, and sustainable power system using BIPV as a model — one that delivers savings in materials and energy efficiency, reduces carbon emissions and environmental pollution, and adds to the aesthetic and architectural appeal of a building."

Itoro Etetim CEO, Metikon Engineering Limited

Nigeria, with its acute power deficit, high diesel costs, and abundant solar irradiance, is arguably one of the markets where this technology makes the most immediate economic sense.

What This Signals

The Sterling Towers project is proof of concept at scale. It demonstrates that a Nigerian engineering firm can deliver Africa's largest BIPV installation to international standards, on a complex urban site, with real operational outcomes. The building has been running since 2022, generating clean energy daily and reducing Sterling Bank's dependence on diesel and grid power.

For Metikon, the project anchors our position as a firm that does not merely install solar panels, but engineers integrated energy systems for buildings. BIPV sits at the intersection of power systems engineering, materials science, and architectural design — precisely the space where we operate.

Cities across Africa will need to decarbonise their building stock. The technology exists. The economics are viable. Sterling Towers is standing evidence.
